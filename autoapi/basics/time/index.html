basics.time.pretty_print_timedelta(t, max_components=None, max_decimal_places=1)[source]

Print a pretty string for a timedelta. For example datetime.timedelta(days=2, seconds=17280) will be printed as ‘2 days, 4 hours, 48 minutes’. Setting max_components to e.g. 1 will change this to ‘2.2 days’, where the number of decimal points can also be set.
